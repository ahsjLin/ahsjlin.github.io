basic

\( f'(a)= \) \( \lim_{h\rightarrow 0} \frac{f(a+h)-f(a)}{(a+h)-a} = \lim_{h\rightarrow 0} \frac{f(a+h)-f(a)}{h} \)
\( \lim_{\Delta x\rightarrow 0} \frac{\Delta y}{\Delta x} \) \( = \lim_{x_2\rightarrow x_1} \frac{f(x_2)-f(x_1)}{x_2-x_1} \)

Leibniz notation

\( f'(a)= \) \( \frac{dy}{dx} |_{x=a} \) or \( \frac{dy}{dx} ]_{x=a} \)

fail to be differentiable

  • A corner
  • A discontinuity
  • A vertical tangent

the product rule

\( (fg)'=fg'+f'g \)

the quotient rule

\( (\frac{f}{g})' = \frac{gf'-fg'}{g^2} \)

the power rule

\( \frac{d}{dx}(x^n) = nx^{n-1} \)

trigonometirc function

\( (\sin x )' = \cos x \)
\( (\cos x )' = -\sin x \)
\( (\tan x )' = \sec^2 x \)

\( (\cot x )' = -\csc^2x \)
\( (\sec x )' = \sec x \tan x \)
\( (\csc x )' = -\csc x \cot x \) 		hint:
\( \tan x = \frac{\sin x}{\cos x} \)
\( \sec x = \frac{1}{\cos x} \)
\( \csc x = \frac{1}{\sin x} \)

the chain rule

\( \frac{dy}{dx} = \frac{dy}{du} \frac{du}{dx} \)
