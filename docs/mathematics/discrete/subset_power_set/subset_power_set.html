Operation properties

\( A,B,C\subseteq U \)

associative laws

\( A\cup(B\cup C) = (A\cup B)\cup C \)
\( A\cap(B\cap C) = (A\cap B)\cap C \)

distributive laws

\( A\cup(B\cap C) = (A\cup B)\cap (A\cup C) \)
\( A\cap(B\cup C) = (A\cap B)\cup (A\cap C) \)

identity laws

\( A\cup\emptyset=A \)
\( A\cap U=A \)

inverse laws

\( A\cup \overline{A}=U \)
\( A\cap\overline{A}=\emptyset \)

domination laws

\( A\cup U=U \),
\( A\cap\emptyset =\emptyset \)

absorption laws

\( A\cup(A\cap B)=A \),
\( A\cap(A\cup B)=A \)

De Morgan's law

  • \( \overline{A\cup B}= \overline{A}\cap\overline{B} \)
  • \( \overline{A\cap B}= \overline{A}\cup\overline{B} \)

proof \( \overline{A\cup B}= \overline{A}\cap\overline{B} \)

(\( \leftrightarrow \))
\( \forall x\in U\)
\( x\in\overline{A\cup B} \)
\( \leftrightarrow x\not\in (A\cup B) \)
\( \leftrightarrow x\not\in A \cup x\not\in B \)
\( \leftrightarrow x\in \overline{A}\cap \overline{B} \)
\( \therefore \overline{A\cup B} = \overline{A}\cap\overline{B} \)

Power set

power set(90 交大題目) Suppose \( A \) is a set.
Sign as \( \mathcal{P}(A) \text{ or } 2^A \)

Definition

\( \mathcal{P}(A) = \{ X|X\subseteq A \} \)
means a set have all of subset of \( A \).
example:
\( A=\{1,2,\{1,3\}\} \)
\( \mathcal{P}(A)=\{\;\)
\( \emptyset \)
\( 1,2,\{1,3\},\)
\( \{1,2\},\{1,\{1,3\}\}, \{2,\{1,3\}\},\)
\( \{1,2,\{1,3\}\} \;\)
\(\} \)

Cardinality of power set

Suppose \( |A|=n \)
\( |\mathcal{P}(A)| =2^n \)

Proof

hint: binomial theorem
\( (x+y)^n= \sum_{i=0}^{n} \begin{pmatrix} n \\ i \end{pmatrix} x^{n-i}y^{i} \)
\( |A|=n \)
\( \forall i=0,1,\dots, n \)
\( S_i\in \mathcal{P}(A) \)
\( S_i\subseteq A \)
\( |S_i|= \begin{pmatrix} n \\ i \end{pmatrix} \)
\( \sum_{i=0}^{n}|S_i| = |\mathcal{P}(A)| = \begin{pmatrix} n \\ 0 \end{pmatrix} + \begin{pmatrix} n \\ 1 \end{pmatrix} + \begin{pmatrix} n \\ 2 \end{pmatrix} + \dots + \begin{pmatrix} n \\ n \end{pmatrix} = \sum_{i=0}^{n} \begin{pmatrix} n \\ i \end{pmatrix} \)
** using binomial theorem
** substitute \( (x,y)=(1,1) \)
\( (1+1)^n= \sum_{i=0}^{n} \begin{pmatrix} n \\ i \end{pmatrix} 1^{n-i}1^{i} = \sum_{i=0}^{n} \begin{pmatrix} n \\ i \end{pmatrix} =2^n \)

Union and Interaction with power set

Example

Sets \( A,B\subseteq U \)
  • \( \mathcal{P}(A\cup B) \neq \mathcal{P}(A) \cup \mathcal{P}(B) \)
    Proof
    Suppose \( |A|=m, |B|=n, \text{ and } A\cap B = \emptyset \)
    \( \rightarrow |A\cup B| = m+n \)
    \( \therefore |\mathcal{P}(A\cup B)|= 2^{m+n} =2^m\cdot 2^n \)
    \( |\mathcal{P}(A)\cup\mathcal{P}(B)|= |\mathcal{P}(A)|+|\mathcal{P}(B)| =2^m + 2^n \)
    \( \rightarrow 2^m\cdot 2^n \neq 2^m + 2^n \)
    \( \therefore \mathcal{P}(A\cup B) \neq \mathcal{P}(A) \cup \mathcal{P}(B) \)
  • \( \mathcal{P}(A\cap B) = \mathcal{P}(A) \cap \mathcal{P}(B) \)
    Proof
    \( S\in \mathcal{P}(A\cap B)\)
    \( \leftrightarrow S\subseteq A\cap B \)
    \( \leftrightarrow S\subseteq A \cap S\subseteq B \)
    \( \leftrightarrow S\in \mathcal{P}(A) \cap S\in \mathcal{P}(B) \)
    \( \leftrightarrow S\in\mathcal{P}(A) \cap \mathcal{P}(B) \)
    \( \therefore \mathcal{P}(A\cap B)= \mathcal{P}(A) \cap \mathcal{P}(B) \)
